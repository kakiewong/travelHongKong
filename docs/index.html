Arrive in style

Start your visit in style. Visitors travelling by plane will arrive at one of the finest airports in the world. Home to world-class brands and dining, it is the perfect place to begin and end your trip to Hong Kong

Three ways to travel

Visitors have a variety of options to choose from when travelling to and from Hong Kong International Aiport. The three main modes of transport are:

  • Airport Express
  • Public buses
  • Taxis

Stay in luxury

Hong Kong is home to some of the finest hotels in the world. Treat yourself to a luxurious stay while you are visiting. Spend a relaxing day at the spa or enjoy a bit room service after a long day of exploring. There are plenty of options to choose from.

Home away from home

There are no shortages of excellent hotels to choose from. Pamper yourself at the Mandarin Oriental. Treat yourself to world-class room service while staying at Peninsula. Experience a luxurious spa treatment at the Shangri-La. Those are just a few of the many hotels visitors can choose from while visiting Hong Kong.

Taste a world of flavor

From Michelin star restaurant in Central to a hawker stands in Mong Kok, visitors can find some of the best food in world in Hong Kong. Chinese, Indian, French, Italian...there is a world of flavor to explore.

Foodie paradise

From the pickiest to the most adventurous eater, there's something for everyone in Hong Kong. Try some traditional dimsum at Tim Ho Wan. Or if you are feeling fancy, you dine in one of the many Michelin star restaurants in the city. Ando and Ta Vie are just two of the many great restaurants you can find in the city.

Discover new treasures

Find the perfect souvenir. There is no shortage of good shopping in Hong Kong. Visitors will find all of the world's top brands in malls throughout the city. High-end fashion not your thing? Then discover new treasures at the outdoor markets in Mong Kok, a buzzing maze of narrow streets known for shopping.

Shop till you drop

Visitors looking for the latest and greatest should make a stop to the iconic Sogo in Causeway Bay. Sneaker heads will feel right at home on Sneaker Street in Mong Kok. If consumer electronics is what you are in the market for then a stop at the Electronics market in Sham Shui Po is a must.
